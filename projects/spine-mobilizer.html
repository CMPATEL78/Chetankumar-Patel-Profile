Spine Mobilization Device

The only handheld spine mobilization device of its kind globally — commercialized and currently available in market.

★ Commercialized Patent IN 202221059481 Published Oct 2022 ★ High UAE Relevance DST Nidhi ITB Grant ✓

Problem Statement

Physiotherapists performing spinal mobilization techniques must apply controlled, repetitive force to specific vertebral joints using their hands. This is physically demanding — causing occupational musculoskeletal strain in the physiotherapist's wrists, thumbs, and shoulders over time — while also making it difficult to apply precise, consistent force across patient sessions.

Existing commercial tools for spinal mobilization are either large clinical machines (expensive, immobile) or generic massage devices that do not deliver the specific force vectors required for therapeutic spinal joint mobilization. No handheld, purpose-built device existed in the market at the time of filing.

The Innovation

A compact, handheld device designed specifically for physiotherapists to apply controlled, adjustable mobilization force to spinal joints. The device features:

  • Dual hook arms that cradle the spinous processes of adjacent vertebrae
  • Central threaded rod with knurled grip for controlled force application
  • Adjustable arm spacing to accommodate different spinal levels and patient anatomy
  • Ergonomic grip design reducing physiotherapist wrist strain
  • Lightweight construction suitable for home visits and clinic use

Methodology

The project followed a structured design-to-market process:

  • Literature review — existing spinal mobilization techniques and tools
  • Physiotherapist interviews — understanding force requirements, grip preferences, and clinical workflow
  • CAD design and iterative prototyping using 3D printing
  • Ergonomics testing with volunteer physiotherapists
  • Patent filing (October 2022) + two design registrations (February 2023)
  • DST Nidhi ITB startup grant application and award
  • Manufacturing setup and commercial launch

  • UAE has 900+ registered physiotherapy clinics — immediate addressable market

  • Target partners: Aster, NMC Health, Burjeel, and Mediclinic hospital chains
  • Technology transfer pathway: licensing or manufacturing partnership with UAE medical device distributor
